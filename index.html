Lebanon, located on the eastern coast of the Mediterranean Sea, is a small yet culturally rich country in the Middle East. Known for its diverse history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cities, Lebanon offers a unique blend of ancient ruins, modern architecture, and scenic beauty. The capital city, Beirut, serves as a bustling cultural hub, renowned for its lively nightlife, eclectic cuisine, and historical landmarks. Throughout its history, Lebanon has been influenced by various civilizations, including Phoenician, Roman, Byzantine, Arab, and Ottoman, resulting in a rich tapestry of traditions and heritage. Despite its relatively small size, Lebanon boasts a diverse topography, ranging from the fertile Bekaa Valley and picturesque Mediterranean coastline to the rugged mountains of Mount Lebanon. This diversity provides ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as skiing, hiking, and beach outings. However, Lebanon also faces challenges, including political instability, economic hardships, and social tensions. Despite these challenges, the resilience and spirit of the Lebanese people continue to shine through, making Lebanon a fascinating and dynamic destination for travelers and a testament to the enduring human spirit.

Our Flag and his history

In 1943 Lebanon gained independence and adopted a flag made up of a white field with two horizontal red stripes at the top and bottom with a green cedar tree in the centre. The red represent the blood shed and white represents purity and peace. The cedar represents the bible.
